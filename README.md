# Macro LLM Tournament

This repository contains a runnable 200-household LLM economy. Real SCE histories
supply household priors, matched SCF records supply continuous financial states,
GPT-5.5 writes conditional household policies in dollars, and ordinary code settles
budgets and counterparties. A small producer turns household demand into output,
inventory, employment, wages, and family income before the households decide again.

The latest anchor-free evaluation is a clean negative on forecast value. Its
predicted-versus-first-release-actual panel covers nine macro variables across four
retrospective monthly origins. Only executed nominal household consumption has a close
aggregate counterpart: nominal PCE. On that central comparison, the household economy
has RMSE `0.782` percentage points and gets one direction right, while the
origin-visible PCE drift supplied to every household has RMSE `0.243`, correlation
`0.974`, and 4/4 direction. The economy is genuinely generated by household actions,
but the household layer has not added signal beyond the information in its prompt.

## Active Economy

```text
200 anonymized SCE histories + matched SCF financial states
                              +
                 public information known at the origin
                              |
                              v
                one isolated GPT-5.5 call per household
                              |
                              v
       beliefs + employed/not-employed policies in nominal dollars
                              |
                              v
          household budgets, credit, and settlement
                              |
                              v
                    population-weighted demand
                              |
                              v
     producer output + inventory -> employment + wages -> family income
                              |
                              v
               fresh period-two household decisions
```

Each rolling origin receives the latest SCE observations that would have been public
at that date. The public information card includes recent aggregate spending as
context, but it does not add that growth to household spending before the LLM acts.
The producer is deliberately mechanical. There are no LLM firms or banks.

## Nine-Variable Prediction Surface

The current panel shows predictions, origin-visible baselines, and first-release
actuals for nominal PCE, PCEPI, real PCE, real disposable income, payrolls,
unemployment, personal saving, revolving credit, and retail sales. It expands
observability; it does not turn every plotted series into an equally valid macro
forecast target.

| Model output | First-release comparator | Interpretation boundary |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Nominal consumption growth | nominal PCE | closest aggregate proxy; the primary result |
| Price growth | PCEPI | household-belief/deflation proxy |
| Real consumption growth | real PCE | belief/deflation proxy |
| Real disposable income growth | real disposable income | belief/deflation proxy |
| Payroll growth; unemployment level | PAYEMS; UNRATE | target-month producer-plan proxies using origin inventory |
| Saving-rate change | PSAVERT | gross household budget-residual proxy, not national saving |
| Revolving-credit growth | revolving credit | direction-only proxy |
| Retail-sales growth | retail sales | declared demand proxy |

All nine retrospective comparisons have `n=4`; they are development diagnostics, not
confirmatory evidence. The secondary rows show where the household beliefs, accounting
residual, and declared producer plan land relative to first releases. They do not validate
the belief-to-real mapping, identify a labor market, establish a national-accounts
saving model, or supply incremental forecast evidence. The consumption failure remains
the central finding.

## Run It

```bash
make ecology-history
make ecology-current-replay
make ecology-retrospective-replay
make ecology-feedback-replay
make ecology-observability
make check
make test
```

Fresh model calls use Codex CLI only:

```bash
make ecology-live-200
make ecology-retrospective-live
make ecology-feedback-live
```

Every model call runs in an empty directory without local-file, shell, web, browser,
app, memory, plugin, or subagent access. Replays bind the public history, prompt,
cards, accepted payloads, parent artifacts, source inputs, executable source, and
economic result.

## Evidence Boundary

- January-April 2026 is retrospective development evidence and may be in model
  knowledge.
- The July 2026 origin is frozen for August and remains unscored.
- Household budgets, goods inventory, bank stocks, and named counterparty flows
  reconcile. A firm balance sheet and full external sector are not modeled.
- V22's apparent 4/4 directional result is retired. It inherited origin-visible PCE
  growth in the household spending baseline before the model acted.
- V23 removes that anchor. Its household forecast currently loses to the visible PCE
  context on level, timing, and direction.
- The nine-variable surface is retrospective (`n=4`). August is a frozen,
  unscored prospective panel row.
